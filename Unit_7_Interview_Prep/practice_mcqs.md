## 🔷 Topic 1: Whiteboard Coding & Communication Etiquette

#### Q1. When a technical interviewer presents a coding problem, what is the very first step you should take?
- A) Start writing code on the board immediately to show speed
- B) Ask clarifying questions regarding inputs, bounds, and constraints
- C) Explain the most optimized solution first
- D) Write test cases in silence
- **Answer: ✅ B**
- **Explanation**: Clarifying requirements prevents you from writing code that doesn't fit constraints (e.g., ignoring negative inputs or memory limitations).

#### Q2. During a live-coding session, if you need to think quietly about a complex algorithm step, what is the best practice?
- A) Stay completely silent until you have solved the entire step
- B) Explicitly state that you need a moment of silence to think, and explain what you are mentally calculating
- C) Mumble to yourself
- D) Ask the interviewer to solve the step for you
- **Answer: ✅ B**
- **Explanation**: Letting the interviewer know you need a moment of silence keeps them engaged in your thought process and prevents long, awkward pauses.

#### Q3. What should you do if the interviewer points out a logical bug in your code?
- A) Argue to defend your code, explaining that it works in your environment
- B) Ignore the comment and continue writing the next section
- C) Acknowledge the feedback, trace the code line-by-line using a test case, and work to fix it
- D) Erase the entire code and start over
- **Answer: ✅ C**
- **Explanation**: Interviewers want to see how you respond to feedback and collaborate. Treating bugs as collaborative fixes demonstrates strong teamwork and problem-solving.

#### Q4. Why is it important to explain a brute-force solution before optimizing?
- A) It guarantees you get partial credit
- B) It establishes a baseline complexity and shows that you understand the problem's basic structure
- C) Brute-force is always the final code you write
- D) It wastes time so you write less code
- **Answer: ✅ B**
- **Explanation**: Starting with a brute force solution shows you can solve the problem. It also sets up a baseline for optimization.

#### Q5. In a coding interview, a "dry run" refers to:
- A) Compiling the code on an IDE
- B) Tracing the code line-by-line mentally using a simple test input, tracking variable values on the board
- C) Writing a summary of the algorithm
- D) Reviewing the code after the interview is over
- **Answer: ✅ B**
- **Explanation**: A dry run is a manual walkthrough of your code's execution path. It helps you verify logic and catch bugs before final submission.

## 🔷 Topic 2: Resume Building & ATS Guidelines

#### Q6. What is the recommended length of a technical resume for a university student or entry-level developer?
- A) 1 Page
- B) 2 Pages
- C) 3 Pages
- D) No page limit
- **Answer: ✅ A**
- **Explanation**: Entry-level resumes should be kept to a single page to remain concise and impactful.

#### Q7. Which formatting element can cause an Applicant Tracking System (ATS) to fail to parse your resume?
- A) Dotted lists
- B) Standard fonts like Arial or Calibri
- C) Text boxes, tables, columns, and graphic templates
- D) Clear section headers
- **Answer: ✅ C**
- **Explanation**: Many ATS parsers read left-to-right and cannot parse text boxes, nested tables, or complex graphic layouts, which can lead to layout corruption.

#### Q8. Under the X-Y-Z formula for resume writing, what does "Y" represent?
- A) The actions you took
- B) The quantitative metric used to measure your project's success
- C) The technology stack used
- D) The name of the project
- **Answer: ✅ B**
- **Explanation**: The formula is: Accomplished [X], as measured by [Y], by doing [Z]. "Y" represents the quantifiable metric of success (e.g., *by 35%*, *reducing latency by 2 seconds*).

#### Q9. In the Skills section of a resume, how should tools and languages be listed?
- A) Grouped logically (e.g., Languages: C++, SQL; Tools: Git, Docker)
- B) In a single paragraph, sorted alphabetically
- C) With self-assigned rating stars (e.g., Java: 5/5 stars)
- D) Scattered throughout the projects section only
- **Answer: ✅ A**
- **Explanation**: Logical grouping makes it easy for recruiters and ATS parsers to identify your technical stack. Rating scales (e.g., 4/5 stars) are subjective and should be avoided.

## 🔷 Topic 3: Behavioral Interviews (STAR Method)

#### Q10. What does the "T" represent in the STAR behavioral interviewing method?
- A) Time spent
- B) Task (the challenge or responsibility)
- C) Team dynamics
- D) Technical skills used
- **Answer: ✅ B**
- **Explanation**: STAR stands for **S**ituation, **T**ask, **A**ction, and **R**esult. "T" defines your specific task or challenge.

#### Q11. When answering behavioral questions, you should focus your "Action" description on:
- A) The achievements of the entire team (using "we")
- B) Your specific actions, contributions, and decisions (using "I")
- C) The mistakes made by other team members
- D) The project's technologies
- **Answer: ✅ B**
- **Explanation**: Interviewers want to evaluate *your* behavior and contributions. Using "I" highlights your personal impact, whereas overusing "we" makes it hard to assess your individual role.

#### Q12. A strong "Result" in a STAR response should include:
- A) A simple statement that the project was completed
- B) Quantifiable metrics and what you learned from the experience
- C) A list of all team members who contributed
- D) The grade you received on the project
- **Answer: ✅ B**
- **Explanation**: Metrics (e.g., *"performance improved by 25%"*) make your impact concrete, and sharing what you learned demonstrates self-reflection and growth.

#### Q13. Question: "Tell me about a time you had a conflict with a team member." What is the interviewer trying to evaluate?
- A) Whether you ever make mistakes
- B) Your conflict-resolution skills, empathy, and professional communication
- C) Who was right in the conflict
- D) If you can work completely alone
- **Answer: ✅ B**
- **Explanation**: Conflict is normal in collaborative settings. The interviewer wants to see if you can handle disagreements constructively and professionally.

## 🔷 Topic 4: Service-Oriented IT Company Hiring Patterns

#### Q14. Service-oriented IT companies (e.g., TCS, Wipro, Infosys) prioritize which topics in their technical MCQ rounds?
- A) Advanced system architecture design
- B) Core CS fundamentals (OS scheduling, Network layers, SQL, OOP concepts)
- C) Advanced machine learning algorithms
- D) Compiler design internals
- **Answer: ✅ B**
- **Explanation**: Service companies look for strong foundational knowledge in core CS subjects like operating systems, networking, databases, and OOP.

#### Q15. In service-oriented company coding rounds, what level of problem difficulty is typically expected?
- A) LeetCode Hard (complex DP, graphs)
- B) Basic string and array manipulations (e.g., reversing strings, prime checks, matrix additions)
- C) Advanced geometry algorithms
- D) Multi-threaded systems design
- **Answer: ✅ B**
- **Explanation**: Coding rounds at service companies typically focus on basic array/string manipulation and arithmetic logic.

#### Q16. Which keyword in Java is a common topic in service company interviews to check inheritance limits?
- A) `final`
- B) `static`
- C) `extern`
- D) `auto`
- **Answer: ✅ A**
- **Explanation**: Marking a class as `final` prevents it from being inherited, which is a common topic in Java syntax checks.

#### Q17. During the HR round, what is the best way to handle questions about relocating to a different office location?
- A) Refuse immediately if you prefer your current city
- B) Express flexibility and a willingness to relocate based on project requirements
- C) Demand a higher salary first
- D) Say you will decide after joining
- **Answer: ✅ B**
- **Explanation**: Service companies work with global clients and value flexibility in staffing. Showing a willingness to relocate increases your hiring potential.

## 🔷 Topic 5: Situational Practice Scenarios

#### Q18. You are coding a solution in an interview and realize your current approach is incorrect. What should you do?
- A) Erase everything and stay silent while writing a new solution
- B) Pretend it is correct and hope the interviewer doesn't notice
- C) Explain to the interviewer why the current approach fails, and outline your plan for a corrected approach
- D) Ask to skip the question
- **Answer: ✅ C**
- **Explanation**: Acknowledging mistakes and explaining how you plan to correct them shows strong self-awareness and problem-solving under pressure.

#### Q19. An interviewer asks a question about a technology you have never used. What is the best response?
- A) Lie and say you are an expert in it
- B) Say "I don't know" and remain silent
- C) Acknowledge you haven't used it, relate it to a similar technology you know, and explain how you would learn it
- D) Ask the interviewer to change the question
- **Answer: ✅ C**
- **Explanation**: Showing adaptability and explaining how your existing skills translate to new tools is highly valued by interviewers.

#### Q20. In a behavioral interview, how should you talk about a project that failed?
- A) Blame other team members for the failure
- B) Avoid mentioning it and talk only about successes
- C) Take responsibility, explain what went wrong, and detail what you learned to prevent future failures
- D) Say it was a hardware error
- **Answer: ✅ C**
- **Explanation**: Discussing failures constructively shows maturity and accountability, key traits for any software engineer.

#### Q21. Which layout is most ATS-friendly?
- A) A multi-column layout with color-blocked headers
- B) A single-column, clean text layout with standard headings
- C) A visual infographic style
- D) A scanned image of your printed resume
- **Answer: ✅ B**
- **Explanation**: Single-column layouts are easy for ATS parsers to scan sequentially, ensuring your skills and experience are read correctly.

#### Q22. The STAR method is used to answer which type of interview questions?
- A) Live coding challenges
- B) Behavioral and situational questions
- C) Quantitative aptitude questions
- D) Database query writing tasks
- **Answer: ✅ B**
- **Explanation**: The STAR method provides a structured format to answer questions starting with *"Tell me about a time when..."*.

#### Q23. What should you do if you finish writing your code before the interview time is up?
- A) Say "I'm done" and wait
- B) Dry run the code with a new test case, explain edge cases, and discuss potential optimizations
- C) Ask if you can leave the room
- D) Browse your phone
- **Answer: ✅ B**
- **Explanation**: Validating your code with new test cases and exploring optimizations shows strong attention to detail and proactive communication.

#### Q24. In service company interviews, how should you prepare for the "Aptitude Round"?
- A) Study advanced data structures
- B) Practice quantitative math, logical reasoning, and verbal comprehension
- C) Memorize system calls
- D) Read standard textbooks on databases
- **Answer: ✅ B**
- **Explanation**: The aptitude round evaluates logical and mathematical skills, serving as an initial screening step.

#### Q25. What is the best practice for listing academic degrees on a resume?
- A) List only the school names without dates
- B) State the degree name, university, graduation year, and GPA
- C) List all high school grades in detail
- D) Leave it out if you have projects
- **Answer: ✅ B**
- **Explanation**: Listing your degree, university, GPA, and graduation year clearly establishes your academic credentials.

#### Q26. Why should you avoid subjective rating scales like "C++: 9/10" on your resume?
- A) It takes up too much space
- B) It is subjective and can lead to unrealistic expectations or arbitrary downgrades by interviewers
- C) ATS cannot read numbers
- D) It violates security protocols
- **Answer: ✅ B**
- **Explanation**: Numerical ratings are subjective. Instead of rating yourself, let your projects and experience demonstrate your proficiency.

#### Q27. In a technical interview, what does it mean to "think aloud"?
- A) Speaking continuously about unrelated topics
- B) Verbalizing your logical steps, assumptions, and choices while solving the problem
- C) Reading the code syntax aloud as you write it
- D) Asking the interviewer questions constantly
- **Answer: ✅ B**
- **Explanation**: Thinking aloud allows the interviewer to follow your problem-solving process and guide you if you get stuck.

#### Q28. What should you do if you get stuck on a coding problem and cannot find a solution?
- A) Give up and end the interview
- B) Stay silent and stare at the board
- C) Explain your current roadblock to the interviewer, summarize what you have tried, and ask for a small hint
- D) Start guessing randomly
- **Answer: ✅ C**
- **Explanation**: Sharing your roadblock and asking for a hint shows strong collaboration and problem-solving skills, and interviewers are usually happy to help.

#### Q29. When describing a project, you should always highlight:
- A) Only the technologies used
- B) The problem solved, your specific contributions, and the final outcome/metrics
- C) The names of all your teammates
- D) The size of the code base
- **Answer: ✅ B**
- **Explanation**: Highlighting the problem, your contributions, and the outcome provides a complete picture of your project impact.

#### Q30. Service IT companies value which soft skill highly during HR rounds?
- A) Advanced public speaking
- B) Flexibility, communication, and a positive attitude toward learning new technologies
- C) Ability to work without supervision
- D) Expert negotiation skills
- **Answer: ✅ B**
- **Explanation**: Adaptability, clear communication, and a willingness to learn are key qualities for consultants working with diverse clients.
